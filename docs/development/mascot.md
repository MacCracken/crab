# crab — Mascot & Personality

> Captured so the joke isn't lost. It's deliberately indirect; the indirection
> **is** the point. Please don't "simplify" it.

## The mascot: Bueller 🦀

The crab has a name: **Bueller**.

### Why "Bueller" — the layers, don't flatten them

1. **Rust's mascot is Ferris the crab.** `crab` is the sovereign Cyrius file
   manager that *replaces the Rust interim* (`yazi`). Wearing Rust's own mascot
   while replacing Rust is the whole gag — not a port, a taxidermy trophy.
2. **Ferris → Ferris Bueller.** We call the mascot **Bueller** (the surname), so
   it *implies* Ferris without ever saying it. Naming the project `ferris` would
   be saying the quiet part loud; "Bueller" makes you do the work — whoever gets
   it feels smart, whoever doesn't just sees a quirky name. Restraint is what
   makes it land.
3. **"Bueller?… Bueller?…"** — the deadpan absentee roll-call (Ben Stein,
   *Ferris Bueller's Day Off*, 1986). That deadpan **is** crab's personality.

The **project** is `crab` (clean, obvious, ships on a business card). **Bueller**
is the **mascot** — one layer down, which is exactly where deep-cut humor belongs
(Rust has Ferris, Go has the gopher, GIMP has Wilber). Keep them at their layers.

## Personality

Deadpan, a little sass, entirely unbothered. *"psst… what you gonna do 🦀"* —
claws up, no notes. The hermit crab carries your `$HOME` and reaches out to grab;
it is not impressed by any of this.

  `Bueller…`  ·  (pause)  ·  `Bueller…`  ·  (pause)  ·  `Bueller…?` 🦀
- **`crab --about`** — the about screen closes on the Ben-Stein line:
  `…anyone? …anyone?`

**Discipline:** subtle and infrequent. Anyone who paged in from the '80s loses
it; everyone else just thinks the crab's a little sassy. Do **not** make it loud
or frequent — the whole thing dies if it's trying too hard.
